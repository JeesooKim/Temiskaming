Communications

Communications receives and transfers incoming telephone calls and locates, by pager, all on-call staff and physicians inside and outside the Hospital. This service provides information and direction to all patients, staff and visitors, and is the control point for most alarm systems within the Hospital. In addition, Communications staff admit approximately 2,200 for inpatient services every year. They also register more than 5,000 people for outpatient services and, with the assistance of the Emergency Room Ward Clerk, another 25,000 people for emergency services.

Ensuring the accuracy of patient information is one very important element of the job for Communications staff. This service area is staffed employees, who provide coverage twenty-four hours per day, seven days per week.

 

Diagnostic Imaging Services

Diagnostic Imaging Services is responsible for performing a wide variety of specialty imaging exams to aid physicians in the detection, diagnosis and treatment of disease.

Diagnostic tests are provided in the following areas: Cat Scan General Radiology Fluoroscopy General Ultrasound Echocardiography Vascular Ultrasound Mammography Bone Mineral Density Stress testing Electrocardiogram The department is linked in partnership with Sudbury as a certified Ontario Breast Screening Site. Full services are provided 8:00 am – 4:00 pm daily Monday to Friday. On-call services are available after hours and on weekends.

 

Discharge Planning

The Discharge Planning function is carried out by the Patient Services Coordinator. The Patient Services Coordinator is responsible for implementing timely discharge plans that enhance continuity of care and efficient use of hospital and community resources. She also recommends options for continuing care and refers the patient to accommodation, programs and services that best meet their needs and preferences. Annually, the Patient Services Coordinator assists in the completion of the placement paperwork for approximately 50 people, arranging case conferences concerning these patients as well.

The person working as the Patient Services Coordinator is shared jointly by Temiskaming Hospital and the Community Care Access Centre. Through this partnership, the Coordinator is also able to ensure appropriate linkages with CCAC services, and refers roughly 300 people a year in this way.
